Wavelength vs Frequency Lab Results

When a student oscillates a rope faster, the frequency increases and the wavelength decreases. This shows an inverse relationship between wavelength and frequency.

To test this, we graph wavelength vs the inverse of frequency (1/f):

Wavelength-Frequency Relationship

The graph of wavelength (λ) vs 1/frequency (1/f) is linear:
\[ \lambda = (\text{slope}) \cdot \frac{1}{f} \]
Since 1/f is the period (T), this is:
\[ \lambda = (\text{slope}) \cdot T \]
The slope has units of m/s, which are the units of velocity (v):
λ=vT \lambda = vT
Rearranged:
v=λf v = \lambda f

Key point: The velocity of the traveling wave is constant and independent of both wavelength and frequency.

Example 1: A student doubles the frequency (from f to 2f).
a) Wavelength: Halves (since v = λf and v is constant).
b) Period: Halves (period and frequency are inverses).
c) Wave speed: Remains unchanged.

Example 2: A student creates 15 waves in 3.0 s; wavelength is 1.2 m.
f=153.0=5Hz f = \frac{15}{3.0} = 5\,\text{Hz} v=λf=1.2m×5Hz=6.0m/s v = \lambda f = 1.2\,\text{m} \times 5\,\text{Hz} = 6.0\,\text{m/s}
