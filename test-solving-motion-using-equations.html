Solving Motion Problems Using Kinematic Equations

If you prefer algebraic number crunching over a graph, then you are a rare person, but let's work through a few examples anyway!

The three kinematic equations (for constant acceleration) that relate the variables of motion are:

  • \[ \Delta x = \frac{1}{2} a t^2 + v_0 t \]
  • \[ v = a t + v_0 \]
  • \[ v^2 = 2a \Delta x + v_0^2 \]

How to solve algebraically:

  • Identify the variables you are given.
  • Identify the variable you are solving for.
  • Pick an equation that allows you to solve for the unknown based on what you know.

Example: (Medium)

Problem: A car accelerates from rest at a rate of 15 m/s² for a distance of 20.0 m.
a) How much time is spent accelerating?
b) What is the final velocity of the car?

a) To solve for time, we need an equation that includes t. Since we don't know the final velocity (v), use:
\[ \Delta x = \frac{1}{2} a t^2 + v_0 t \] \[ 20 = 0.5 \times 15 \times t^2 \] \[ 20 = 7.5 t^2 \] \[ t = 1.6\,\text{s} \]

b) To solve for final velocity, use an equation that includes v. We'll use:
\[ v = a t + v_0 \] \[ v = 15 \times 1.6 + 0 = 24\,\text{m/s} \]
Note: This equation uses the answer from part (a). If you make a mistake in part (a), it will affect part (b).

Example: (Hard)

Problem: An object traveling at 30 m/s slows to a stop over a distance of 100 m. How much time does it take for the object to stop?

We know initial velocity (30 m/s), final velocity (0 m/s), and displacement (100 m). We need time. However, all of the kinematic equations require us to know the acceleration before we can solve for anything else. This will be a two-part problem: first find acceleration, then solve for time.

Step 1: Find acceleration.
\[ v^2 = 2a \Delta x + v_0^2 \] \[ 0 = 2a(100) + (30)^2 \] \[ 0 = 200a + 900 \] \[ a = -4.5\,\text{m/s}^2 \]

Step 2: Find time.
Use either of the remaining equations. We'll use:
v=at+v0 v = a t + v_0 \[ 0 = -4.5 t + 30 \] \[ t = 6.7\,\text{s} \]

Tip: For multi-step problems, always solve for acceleration first if it's not given, then use it to find the desired variable.
