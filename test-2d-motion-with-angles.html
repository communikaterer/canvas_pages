Projectile Motion with Angles

When the starting velocity is not entirely horizontal, such as kicking a ball at 15 m/s at a 60° angle, the initial motion is both horizontal and vertical. Which equation do you use?

No vertical motion can be used in the horizontal equation and no horizontal motion belongs in the vertical equation.

Instead, break the initial velocity vector into its horizontal and vertical components. Use these components in their respective equations:

  • Horizontal: \[ v_x = v \cos\theta \]
  • Vertical: \[ v_y = v \sin\theta \]
Example 1:
A student kicks a ball on the ground so that the ball has an initial speed of 15 m/s at an angle of 60°. What is the horizontal distance traveled by the ball while it’s in the air?
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Break velocity into components

\[ v_x = 15\cos 60^\circ = 7.5\,\text{m/s} \]
\[ v_y = 15\sin 60^\circ = 13\,\text{m/s} \]

Step 2: Find time in the air (vertical motion)
\[ 0 = \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)t^2 + (13)t \]
\[ 0 = t(-4.9t + 13) \]
\[ t = 0 \text{ or } t = 2.7\,\text{s} \]
(Ignore t = 0; total time in air is 2.7 s)

Step 3: Find horizontal distance
\[ x = v_x t = (7.5\,\text{m/s})(2.7\,\text{s}) = 20.3\,\text{m} \]
The ball will land 20.3 m to the right of where it started.

Example 2:
A student kicks a ball off the ground at an angle of 35°. The ball travels 8.5 meters horizontally in 0.8 seconds. What was the initial velocity of the ball? What was the maximum height of the ball?
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Find horizontal velocity
\[ x = v_{x} t \] \[ 8.5 = v_{x}(0.8) \implies v_{x} = 10.6\,\text{m/s} \]
Step 2: Find initial velocity (hypotenuse)
\[ v_{x} = v \cos 35^\circ \] \[ v = \frac{10.6}{\cos 35^\circ} = 13\,\text{m/s} \]
Step 3: Find max height (vertical motion)
\[ v_{y} = v \sin 35^\circ = 13 \times 0.574 = 7.46\,\text{m/s} \]
At max height, vertical velocity = 0: \[ v^2 = v_{0y}^2 + 2a y \] \[ 0 = (7.46)^2 + 2(-9.8)y \] \[ y = \frac{-(7.46)^2}{2(-9.8)} = 2.8\,\text{m} \]
Initial velocity: 13 m/s. Max height: 2.8 m.

Example 3:
A student standing on top of a 20 m tall building throws a rock off the roof with an initial velocity of 12 m/s at an angle of 50°. What is the horizontal distance traveled by the rock? (Neglect air resistance.)
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Find vertical velocity component
\[ v_{y} = 12\sin 50^\circ = 9.2\,\text{m/s} \]
Step 2: Use vertical motion to solve for time in air
\[ y = \frac{1}{2} a t^2 + v_{0y} t + y_0 \] \[ 0 = -4.9 t^2 + 9.2 t + 20 \]
Use quadratic formula: \[ t = \frac{-9.2 \pm \sqrt{(9.2)^2 - 4(-4.9)(20)}}{2(-4.9)} \] t=3.2s t = 3.2\,\text{s}
Step 3: Find horizontal velocity component
vx=12cos50∘=7.7m/s v_{x} = 12\cos 50^\circ = 7.7\,\text{m/s}
Step 4: Find horizontal distance
x=vxt=(7.7)(3.2)=24.7m x = v_{x} t = (7.7)(3.2) = 24.7\,\text{m}
The rock lands 24.7 m from the base of the building.
