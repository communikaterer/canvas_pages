Solving Motion Problems Using Velocity Graphs

You can answer almost all questions involving motion with constant acceleration using velocity vs. time graphs. Remember, velocity graphs do not provide position data directly, but you can find displacement by calculating the area under the curve. Your sketch does not need to be highly detailed or perfectly scaled—just label the important points!

Example (Easy)

Problem: A student starts from rest and travels forwards for 10 seconds. Their final velocity is 3.0 m/s.
a) What is the student's acceleration?
b) How far did the student travel?

Solution: Sketch and label a simple velocity vs time graph.

a) Acceleration is the slope of the velocity vs time graph:
\[ a = \frac{3 - 0}{10 - 0} = 0.3\,\text{m/s}^2 \]

b) Distance traveled is the area under the graph (a triangle):
\[ \Delta x = \frac{1}{2} \times 10\,\text{s} \times 3\,\text{m/s} = 15\,\text{m} \]

Example (Medium)

Problem: A car moving at a velocity of 30 m/s travels 50 m while braking. The final velocity is 20 m/s.
a) How much time did the car brake for?
b) What was the acceleration?

Solution: Sketch and label a velocity vs time graph.

a) The area under the line is the displacement. The area is a rectangle plus a triangle. Let t be the unknown time:
\[ 50 = \frac{1}{2} t (30 - 20) + t (20) \] \[ 50 = 5t + 20t \] \[ 50 = 25t \] \[ t = 2\,\text{s} \]

b) Acceleration is the slope of the line:
\[ a = \frac{20 - 30}{2 - 0} = -5\,\text{m/s}^2 \]

Example (Hard)

Problem: An object accelerates from rest at a rate of 8 m/s² for a distance of 40 m.
a) What is the time traveled?
b) What is the final velocity?

Solution: Start by sketching and labeling a velocity vs time graph.

This is the trickiest type of question to solve using the graph, since we don't know any values on the axes except the starting velocity. (For these, using equations is often easier, but let's solve graphically!)

The slope is acceleration:
\[ 8 = \frac{v}{t} \implies v = 8t \]
The area under the graph (a triangle) is the displacement:
\[ 40 = \frac{1}{2} t v \] Substitute for v:
\[ 40 = \frac{1}{2} t (8t) \] 40=4t2 40 = 4t^2 t=3.2s t = 3.2\,\text{s}
Now plug that time into the velocity equation:
v=8×3.2=25.6m/s v = 8 \times 3.2 = 25.6\,\text{m/s}
