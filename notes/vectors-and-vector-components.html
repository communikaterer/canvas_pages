Vector vs Scalar Measurements

What is a vector measurement?

A vector is any measurement with both a magnitude (number value) and a direction. Vectors can be represented by arrows: the length shows magnitude, the angle shows direction.

Examples of vectors: displacement, velocity, acceleration.

Scalar measurements have only magnitude (no direction).

Examples of scalars: distance, speed, mass, volume, etc.

Vector Components

All vectors can be broken down into horizontal and vertical components (using trigonometry).

Example: A student drives at 60 mi/hr at 30°.

Start by sketching a diagram.



North component:

We know the hypotenuse of this triangle. The north component is opposite of the hypotenuse.

$$ \begin{align*} \sin \theta &= \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}} \\ \sin(30^\circ) &= \frac{\text{north component}}{60 \text{ mi/hr}} \\ 60 \text{ mi/hr}\times \sin(30^\circ) &= \text{north component} \\ \text{north component} &= 30\text{ mi/hr} \end{align*} $$
East component:

We know the hypotenuse of this triangle. The east component is adjacent of the hypotenuse.

\begin{align*} \cos \theta &= \frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}} \\ \cos(30^\circ) &= \frac{\text{east component}}{60\text{ mi/hr}} \\ 60 \text{ mi/hr} \times \cos(30^\circ) &= \text{east component}\\ \text{east component} &= 52 \text{ mi/hr} \end{align*}
The student is traveling 52 mi/hr to the east and 30 mi/hr to the north.

Component Notation

Vector components can be written in several ways:

  • List the components: veast = 30 mi/hr, vnorth = 52 mi/hr
  • Angle brackets: <30, 52> mi/hr
  • Unit vector notation: v = (30 mi/hr) î + (52 mi/hr) ĵ

Using Components to Solve for the Vector

Sometimes, we are given the the measurements in component form and asked to solve for the vector form.

Example: A student walks 40 m west and 20 m south. What is their displacement?

Start by sketching a diagram.



Step 1: Find the magnitude using the Pythagorean Theorem:
$$ \text{displacement} = \sqrt{(40)^2 + (20)^2} = 44.7\,\text{m}
Step 2: Find direction (using trig):
$$ \tan\theta = \frac{20}{40}$$ $$\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.5) = 26.6^\circ $$
Direction: Since both components are negative (west and south), the angle is 206.6° (measured from the positive x-axis, counterclockwise).
