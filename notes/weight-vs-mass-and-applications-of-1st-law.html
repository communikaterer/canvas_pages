Mass vs Weight

Mass is the amount of matter in an object (units: kg).
Weight is the force of gravity on an object (units: Newtons, N).
Weight depends on the gravitational field strength of the planet.

Lab Data Analysis

The relationship between mass and weight is linear: doubling mass doubles weight.

The y-intercept is essentially zero: no mass means no weight.
The slope of the graph is 9.8 N/kg, which is Earth's gravitational field strength (g).
Other planets: Mars: 3.7 N/kg, Moon: 1.6 N/kg.
Weight equation:
\[ W = mg \] or \[ F_g = mg \]
where g is the gravitational field strength (N/kg).

Applications of the Weight Equation

Show/Hide Examples

Example 1: A box has a mass of 15 kg. What is its weight in Newtons and pounds? (4.4 N = 1 lb)
\[ W = mg = 15\,\text{kg} \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} = 147\,\text{N} \]
\[ W = 147\,\text{N} \times \frac{1\,\text{lb}}{4.4\,\text{N}} = 33.4\,\text{lb} \]

Example 2: What is the mass (kg) of a person who weighs 150 lb?
\[ 150\,\text{lb} \times \frac{4.4\,\text{N}}{1\,\text{lb}} = 660\,\text{N} \]
\[ 660\,\text{N} = m \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} \]
\[ m = \frac{660}{9.8} = 67.3\,\text{kg} \]

Example 3: A 2.0 kg book sits on a table. What is the normal force?

\[ W = mg = 2.0\,\text{kg} \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} = -19.6\,\text{N} \]
The normal force must cancel the weight: \[ N = 19.6\,\text{N} \]

Example 4: A 10.0 kg box is pushed with a steady force of 20 N and moves at constant speed. What is the normal force? What is the friction force?

Friction force: To cancel the push, \[ f = -20\,\text{N} \]
Normal force: \[ N = 10.0\,\text{kg} \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} = 98\,\text{N} \]

Tension in Ropes Example

Show/Hide Example

Example: Metal spheres are suspended by ropes. Find the tension in each rope.

Step 1: Analyze the 8 kg sphere.

Tension in lower rope cancels weight: T=8kg×9.8N/kg=78.4N T = 8\,\text{kg} \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} = 78.4\,\text{N}
Step 2: Analyze the whole system for the top rope.

Tension in top rope cancels total weight: T=11kg×9.8N/kg=107.8N T = 11\,\text{kg} \times 9.8\,\text{N/kg} = 107.8\,\text{N}
