Units of Measurement

All SI (System International aka the metric system) units of measurement boil down to various combinations of seven basic units: seconds, kilograms, meters, kelvin, ampere, moles, and candela.

For example, a unit of pressure that you may be familiar with is kPa or kilopascals. A pascal is a unit that is really just a quick way of writing: \( \frac{kg}{s^2 \cdot m} \). Don't worry, you don't need to know that for the test, it's just cool.

Practice

  1. How many inches are in 0.25 miles? (medium difficulty)
    (5280 ft = 1 mile, 12 in = 1 ft)
  2. How many cubic meters does 805 g of a material occupy if its density is 0.15 g/mL? (hard)
    (1 mL = 1 cubic centimeter)

Answers:

  1. 15,840 inches
  2. 0.0054 m³
