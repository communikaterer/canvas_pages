Examples with Solutions

Example 1

A person travels 60 miles in 1 hour, stops the car to take a 10-minute break, and then drives 30 miles in 10 minutes.
What is the average speed for this trip?

Click here to see the solution
The total distance is 60 miles + 30 miles = 90 miles.
The total time is 60 minutes + 10 minutes + 10 minutes = 80 minutes.
The average speed is (90 miles / 80 minutes) = 1.13 miles per minute.
Note that we can't determine the average velocity because we don't know the displacement, only the distance.

Part b) The total distance traveled is 5 m forward + 3 m backward = 8 meters.
The total time is 20 seconds.
The average speed is:
\[ \frac{8 \, \text{meters}}{20 \, \text{seconds}} = 0.4 \, \text{m/s}. \]

The total displacement is: \[ (+5 \, \text{m}) + (-3 \, \text{m}) = 2 \, \text{m} \quad \text{(the object ended 2 meters in front of the starting position)}. \]
The average velocity is: \[ \frac{2 \, \text{m}}{20 \, \text{seconds}} = 0.1 \, \text{m/s}. \]

Example 3

A student runs on a track. Each lap on the track is 0.25 miles. If a student runs 1 lap in 120 seconds, what is the student's average speed and average velocity?

Click here to see the solution
Total distance = 0.25 miles.
Total displacement = 0 miles (they ended back where they started, so there is no difference between ending and starting positions).
Total time = 120 seconds.
The average speed is: \[ \frac{0.25 \, \text{miles}}{120 \, \text{seconds}} = 0.0021 \, \text{miles per second}. \]
The average velocity is: \[ \frac{0 \, \text{miles}}{120 \, \text{seconds}} = 0 \, \text{miles per second}. \]
