Power

Power is the rate of change of energy in a system. It is measured in watts (W), where 1 watt = 1 joule/second. Another common unit is horsepower (hp):
746 W = 1 hp

\[ P = \frac{W}{t} \]

Where P is power (watts), W is work (joules), and t is time (seconds).

Example 1: Lifting a Bucket from a Well
A student uses a motor to pull a 10.0 kg bucket of water out of a 100 m well. The motor has a power rating of 2.0 hp. How long will it take?
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Find work done
\[ W = mgh = 10.0 \times 9.8 \times 100 = 9800\,\text{J} \]
Step 2: Convert power to watts
\[ 2.0\,\text{hp} \times 746\,\text{W/hp} = 1492\,\text{W} \]
Step 3: Solve for time
\[ P = \frac{W}{t} \implies t = \frac{W}{P} = \frac{9800}{1492} = 6.6\,\text{s} \]

Example 2: Car Engine Power
A 1200 kg car accelerates from rest to 30 m/s in 4.0 s. What is the power rating of the car’s engine?
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Find work done (change in kinetic energy)
\[ W = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 0.5 \times 1200 \times (30)^2 = 540000\,\text{J} \]
Step 2: Find power
\[ P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{540000}{4.0} = 135000\,\text{W} \]
Step 3: Convert to horsepower
\[ P = \frac{135000}{746} = 181\,\text{hp} \]

Example 3: Hair Dryer Energy
A hair dryer has a power rating of 1800 W. How much energy is used in 12 minutes?
Show/Hide Answer

Step 1: Convert time to seconds
\[ 12\,\text{min} \times 60 = 720\,\text{s} \]
Step 2: Find energy used
\[ P = \frac{W}{t} \implies W = Pt = 1800 \times 720 = 1.3 \times 10^6\,\text{J} \]

Note: Don't confuse W (watts, unit of power) with W (work, symbol for energy).
Example 4: Energy Units and Cost
What is a kilowatt-hour (kWh)?
1kWh=(1000W)(3600s)=3.6×106J 1\,\text{kWh} = (1000\,\text{W})(3600\,\text{s}) = 3.6 \times 10^6\,\text{J}
If energy costs $0.22 per kWh, you pay $0.22 for every 3.6 million joules of energy.

Example: A computer uses 130 W. How much does it cost to run for 24 hours at $0.26 per kWh?
Show/Hide Answer

Cost=(0.26$/kWh)×(1kW1000W)×130W×24h \text{Cost} = (0.26\,\$/\text{kWh}) \times \left(\frac{1\,\text{kW}}{1000\,\text{W}}\right) \times 130\,\text{W} \times 24\,\text{h}
Cost=$0.81 \text{Cost} = \$0.81
