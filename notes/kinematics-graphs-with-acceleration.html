Kinematic Graphs – Qualitative

We've now analyzed the relationship for position and time for an object moving forwards while speeding up. What do the equations and graphs look like for an object that is slowing down? Or moving backwards?

The equation for position vs time doesn't change for any of these situations. Any object that experiences a constant acceleration (a velocity that changes by the same value every second) will have a position vs time relationship described by:

\[ x = \frac{1}{2} a t^2 + v_0 t + x_0 \]

The position vs time graph will always be parabolic. This may be a top-opening or bottom-opening parabola, and you may only see part of it, but it's always parabolic.

Let's explore position vs time, velocity vs time, and acceleration vs time graphs for various types of motion.

Negative Acceleration

One of the largest misconceptions concerning acceleration is that a negative acceleration indicates that an object is slowing down. This is not always true. Similarly, a positive acceleration does not always mean the object is speeding up.

  • If the direction of motion is the same as the direction of acceleration, the object’s speed will increase. For example, if the object moves forwards and the acceleration is positive or the object moves backwards and the acceleration is negative.
  • If the direction of motion is opposite the direction of acceleration, the object’s speed will decrease. For example, if the object moves forwards and the acceleration is negative or the object moves backwards and the acceleration is positive.
