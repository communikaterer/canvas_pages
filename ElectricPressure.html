Understanding Voltage

‚ö° What is Voltage?

  • Voltage (V) is the difference in electric pressure between two points
  • Measured in Volts (V)
  • Also called "electric potential difference"
  • Related to EMF (electromotive force) in batteries

Note about EMF: For ideal batteries, voltage equals EMF. As batteries age, internal resistance increases, making EMF larger than actual voltage.

üìä Voltage in Series Circuits

Key Principles:

  • Maximum voltage occurs at battery
  • Individual voltages add up to battery voltage
  • Higher resistance = Higher voltage drop

Vbattery = VA + VB + VC

üìä Voltage in Parallel Circuits

Key Principle: Voltage is identical across parallel branches

Vbattery = VA = VB = VC

Note: This is true regardless of component resistance!

Circuit Examples

Series Circuit Example

View Solution

Given: Battery voltage = 15 V

Case 1: Equal resistances

  • Voltage splits equally: 15V = 3V
  • VA = VB = VC = 5 V

Case 2: Bulb C has double resistance

  • 15V = V + V + 2V
  • VA = VB = 3.75 V
  • VC = 7.5 V

Parallel Circuit Example

View Solution

Given: Battery voltage = 15 V

Key Point: In parallel circuits, voltage is the same across all branches

VA = VB = VC = 15 V

This is true regardless of bulb resistances!

Mixed Circuit Example

View Solution

Given: Battery voltage = 15 V

Case 1: Equal resistances

  • First branch: VA = 15 V
  • Second branch: 15V = 2V
  • Therefore: VB = VC = 7.5 V

Case 2: Bulb C has double resistance

  • First branch: VA = 15 V
  • Second branch: 15V = V + 2V
  • Therefore: VB = 5 V, VC = 10 V
