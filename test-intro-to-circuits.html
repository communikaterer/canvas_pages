Key Finding: Only metal objects allow the bulbs to light.

Why? Metals are conductors - they allow electrical charge to move easily through them. Non-metal objects act like breaks in the circuit because they block the flow of charge.

Important: This means electrical circuits must be made ENTIRELY from conductors (metals) to function!

üß™ Understanding Light Bulb Design

Click to Show Explanation (after completing your analysis!)

The Path of Electricity

  1. The filament is the thin wire that actually produces light
  2. Two thicker support wires connect the filament to the bulb's contact points
  3. One wire connects to the bottom tip
  4. One wire connects to the side of the bulb

Key Understanding: The bulb must be connected at BOTH points (tip AND side) to include the filament in the circuit!

Important Note: It's possible to have a complete conducting loop through the metal parts of the bulb without including the filament (for example, by connecting only to the metal bands). While electricity would flow, the bulb wouldn't light because the filament isn't part of the circuit!
